Question 1 (***)[R]#

Water flows through a sluice gate as shown below. The friction losses through the gate are estimated to be 0.2 m. Find the horizontal thrust of the water per meter width on the sluice gate. Given: y1 = 2.2 m, y2 = 0.4 m and y3 = 0.5 m. {12.33 kN to the right}

Given:

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} h_{f} &= 0.2 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &y_{1} &= 2.2 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &y_{2} &= 0.4 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] y_{3} &= 0.5 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Hint

Conservation of mass:

\[\begin{split} Q_1=Q_2\\ V_1A_1=V_2A_2\\ V_1(y_1*b)=V_2(y_2*b)\\ V_1\frac{y_1}{y_2}=V_2\\ 5.5V_1=V_2\\ \end{split}\]

Conservation of energy:

\[\begin{split} z_1+\frac {p_1}{\rho g}+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}=z_2+\frac {p_2}{\rho g}+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}+h_{f}\\ \frac {p_1}{\rho g}= 0, \frac {p_2}{\rho g}=0\\ z_1+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}=z_2+\frac{(5.5V_1)^2}{2g}+h_{f}\\ \end{split}\]

Solving for \(V_1\):

\[ V_1=(\frac{2g(z_1-z_2-h_f}{29.25}))^{0.5} \]

Conservation of Momentum:

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x= \rho Q (V_2x-V_1x)\\ F_1-F_2-R_x=\rho Q (V_2x-V_1x) \end{split}\]

Solution

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} z_{1} &= 2.200 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] z_{2} &= 0.400 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] V_{1} &= \left( \frac{ 2 \cdot g \cdot \left( z_{1} - z_{2} - h_{f} \right) }{ 29.25 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= \left( \frac{ 2 \cdot 9.810 \cdot \left( 2.200 - 0.400 - 0.200 \right) }{ 29.25 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= 1.036 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] V_{2} &= 5.5 \cdot V_{1} \\&= 5.5 \cdot 1.036 \\&= 5.698 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] b &= 1 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] A_{1} &= y_{1} \cdot b \\&= 2.200 \cdot 1 \\&= 2.200 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{2} &= y_{2} \cdot b \\&= 0.400 \cdot 1 \\&= 0.400 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= V_{1} \cdot A_{1} \\&= 1.036 \cdot 2.200 \\&= 2.279 \; \;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot \frac{ y_{1} }{ 2 } \cdot y_{1} - \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot \frac{ y_{2} }{ 2 } \cdot y_{2} - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( V_{2} - V_{1} \right) \\&= 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot \frac{ 2.200 }{ 2 } \cdot 2.200 - 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot \frac{ 0.400 }{ 2 } \cdot 0.400 - 1000 \cdot 2.279 \cdot \left( 5.698 - 1.036 \right) \\&= 12330.415 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x_{}} &= 12.330 \; \;\textrm{(kN)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

The force on the structure is the same size but in the opposite direction:

Question 2 (***)#

Flow occurs over a spillway with a width of 100 m. The spillway has a cross section as shown below. The water depth on the upstream side of the spillway (y1) is 20 m, and on the downstream side (y2) is 1.5 m. The friction energy headloss over the spillway is 1.2 m. Determine the horizontal force of the water on the spillway. {147.7 MN to the right}

Given:

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} b &= 100 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &y_{1} &= 20 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &y_{2} &= 1.500 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \\[10pt] h_{f} &= 1.200 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Hint

The first step is to apply the conservation of mass to establish the relationship between \(V_2\) and \(V_1\)

\[\begin{split} Q_1=Q_2\\ V_1*A_1=V_2*A_2\\ V_1*y_1*b=V_2*y_2*b\\ V_2= \frac{y_1V1}{y_2} \end{split}\]

Replacing the values for this exercise, we have:

\[\begin{split} V_2= \frac{20V_1}{1.5}\\ V_2= 13.33 V_1 \end{split}\]

The next step is use the Bernoulli equation and replace the values of pressure to have the depth of the spillway in each section

\[\begin{split} z_1+\frac {P_1}{\rho g}+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}=z_2+\frac {P_2}{\rho g}+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}+h_{f}\\ \frac {P_1}{\rho g}= y_1 , \frac {P_2}{\rho g}=y_2\\ \end{split}\]

but \(z_1=z_2\) because of horizontal floor

\[\begin{split} \frac{V_1^2}{2g}=y_2+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}+h_{f}-y_1\\ \frac{V_1^2}{2g}=y_2+\frac{(13.33*V_1)^2}{2g}+h_{f}-y_1 \end{split}\]

Once the velocities at both sections are determined, the conservation of momentum can then be applied:

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]
\[\begin{split} \rho gh_{c1}A_1-\rho gh_{c2}A_2-F = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]

The centroid of the spillway is:

\[\begin{split} h_c= \frac{y}{2}\\ \rho g\frac {y_1}{2}A_1-\rho g\frac {y_2}{2}A_2-F = \rho Q (V_2-V_1) \end{split}\]

Solution

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} V_{1} &= \left( \frac{ \left( y_{1} - y_{2} - h_{f} \right) \cdot 2 \cdot g }{ \left( \frac{ y_{1} }{ y_{2} } \right) ^{ 2 } - 1 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= \left( \frac{ \left( 20 - 1.500 - 1.200 \right) \cdot 2 \cdot 9.810 }{ \left( \frac{ 20 }{ 1.500 } \right) ^{ 2 } - 1 } \right) ^{ 0.5 } \\&= 1.386 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] V_{2} &= y_{1} \cdot \frac{ V_{1} }{ y_{2} } \\&= 20 \cdot \frac{ 1.386 }{ 1.500 } \\&= 18.476 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= V_{1} \cdot y_{1} \cdot b \\&= 1.386 \cdot 20 \cdot 100 \\&= 2771.333 \; \;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{1} &= b \cdot y_{1} \\&= 100 \cdot 20 \\&= 2000 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{2} &= b \cdot y_{2} \\&= 100 \cdot 1.500 \\&= 150.000 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] F &= \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot y_{1} \cdot \frac{ A_{1} }{ 2 } - \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot y_{2} \cdot \frac{ A_{2} }{ 2 } - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( V_{2} - V_{1} \right) \\&= 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot 20 \cdot \frac{ 2000 }{ 2 } - 1000 \cdot 9.810 \cdot 1.500 \cdot \frac{ 150.000 }{ 2 } - 1000 \cdot 2771.333 \cdot \left( 18.476 - 1.386 \right) \\&= 147734607.558 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] F_{kN} &= \frac{ F }{ 1000000 } \\&= \frac{ 147734607.558 }{ 1000000 } \\&= 147.735 \; \;\textrm{(MN)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Question 1 (**)[R]#

A nozzle is attached to a pipe as shown below. The inside diameter of the pipe is 100 mm, while the water jet exiting from the nozzle has a diameter of 50 mm. The pressure at section 1 is 500 kPa, and the velocity at sections 1 is 6 m/s. Calculate the force of the water on the nozzle. {3.079 kN to the right}

Given:

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} D_{1} &= 0.100 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &D_{2} &= 0.050 \; \;\textrm{(m)} &V_{1} &= 6 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)} \\[10pt] P_{1} &= 500000 \; \;\textrm{(Pa)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Hint

To calculate the force of the water on the nozzle, the momentum equation can be aplied in the x-Direction:

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ F_{p1}-F_{p2}-R_x=\rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]

Replacing the \(F_{p}\) with \(PA\) we have:

\[\begin{split} P_1A_1-P_2A_2-R_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]

To calculate the flow rate, the conservation of mass can be used

\[\begin{split} Q_1=Q_2\\ V_1A_1=V_2A_2 \end{split}\]

Solution

Show code cell outputs Hide code cell outputs
\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} A_{1} &= \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.100 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 0.008 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= V_{1} \cdot A_{1} \\&= 6 \cdot 0.008 \\&= 0.047 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{2} &= \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{2} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.050 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 0.002 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] V_{2} &= \frac{ Q }{ A_{2} } \\&= \frac{ 0.047 }{ 0.002 } \\&= 24.000 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= P_{1} \cdot A_{1} - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( V_{2} - V_{1} \right) \\&= 500000 \cdot 0.008 - 1000 \cdot 0.047 \cdot \left( 24.000 - 6 \right) \\&= 3078.761 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x_{}} &= \frac{ R_{x} }{ 1000 } \\&= \frac{ 3078.761 }{ 1000 } \\&= 3.079 \; \;\textrm{(kN)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ P_1A_1-P_2A_2-R_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]

a)

Solution

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} V_{2} &= \frac{ V_{1} \cdot \left( \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \right) }{ \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{2} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } } \\&= \frac{ 2.800 \cdot \left( 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.750 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \right) }{ 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.500 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } } \\&= 6.300 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] p_{2} &= \left( \left( \frac{ p_{1} }{ \rho_{water} \cdot g } \right) + \left( \frac{ \left( V_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot g } \right) - \left( \frac{ \left( V_{2} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot g } \right) \right) \cdot \rho_{water} \cdot g \\&= \left( \left( \frac{ 650000 }{ 1000 \cdot 9.810 } \right) + \left( \frac{ \left( 2.800 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot 9.810 } \right) - \left( \frac{ \left( 6.300 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot 9.810 } \right) \right) \cdot 1000 \cdot 9.810 \\&= 634075.000 \; \;\textrm{(Pa)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{1} &= \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.750 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 0.442 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{2} &= \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{2} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.500 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 0.196 \; \;\textrm{(m2)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= V_{1} \cdot A_{1} \\&= 2.800 \cdot 0.442 \\&= 1.237 \; \;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= p_{1} \cdot A_{1} - p_{2} \cdot A_{2} - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( V_{2} - V_{1} \right) \\&= 650000 \cdot 0.442 - 634075.000 \cdot 0.196 - 1000 \cdot 1.237 \cdot \left( 6.300 - 2.800 \right) \\&= 158331.361 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x_{}} &= \frac{ R_{x} }{ 1000 } \\&= \frac{ 158331.361 }{ 1000 } \\&= 158.331 \; \;\textrm{(kN)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Question 3 (***)#

A perforated plate with 50% of its area consisting of small holes is installed at the outlet of a 30 cm diameter horizontal water pipe carrying water at a velocity of 2 m/s. Neglect energy losses and calculate the force of the water on the perforated plate. \[ \begin{aligned} V_{1} &= 2 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)} &D_{1} &= 0.300 \; \;\textrm{(m)} \end{aligned} \]

\[\begin{split} V_1*A_1=V_2*0.5*A_1\\ V_2=\frac {V_2}{0.5} \end{split}\]

\[\begin{split} z_1+\frac {P_1}{\rho g}+\frac{V_1^2}{2g}=z_2+\frac {P_2}{\rho g}+\frac{V_2^2}{2g}\\ \end{split}\]

where \(z_1= z_2\), and \(P_2= 0 Pa\)

\[ \frac {P_1}{\rho g}=\frac{V_2^2}{2g}-\frac{V_1^2}{2g} \]

Once the pressure in section one is calculated, the force of the water can be calculated with the conservation of Momentum:

\[\begin{split} \sum F_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ P_1A_1-P_2A_2-R_x = \rho Q (V_2-V_1)\\ \end{split}\]

where \(P_2\) is atmospheric pressure= 0

\[\begin{split} R_x = -\rho Q (V_2-V_1)+P_1A_1\\ \end{split}\]

Solution

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} V_{2} &= \frac{ V_{1} }{ 0.5 } \\&= \frac{ 2 }{ 0.5 } \\&= 4.000 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] A_{1} &= \pi \cdot \frac{ \left( D_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 3.142 \cdot \frac{ \left( 0.300 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 4 } \\&= 0.071 \\[10pt] \\[10pt] Q &= V_{1} \cdot A_{1} \\&= 2 \cdot 0.071 \\&= 0.141 \; \;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] P_{1} &= \left( \frac{ \left( V_{2} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot g } - \frac{ \left( V_{1} \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot g } \right) \cdot g \cdot \rho_{water} \\&= \left( \frac{ \left( 4.000 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot 9.810 } - \frac{ \left( 2 \right) ^{ 2 } }{ 2 \cdot 9.810 } \right) \cdot 9.810 \cdot 1000 \\&= 6000.000 \; \;\textrm{(Pa)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= P_{1} \cdot A_{1} - \rho_{water} \cdot Q \cdot \left( V_{2} - V_{1} \right) \\&= 6000.000 \cdot 0.071 - 1000 \cdot 0.141 \cdot \left( 4.000 - 2 \right) \\&= 141.372 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

The force on the plate is 141.4 N to the right

\;\textrm{(m3/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{x} &= \left( - \rho_{water} \right) \cdot Q_{A} \cdot \left( \left( - v_{1} \right) \cdot \cos \left( \operatorname{radians} \theta \right) - v_{1} \right) - \rho_{water} \cdot Q_{B} \cdot \left( v_{1} \cdot \cos \left( \operatorname{radians} \theta \right) - v_{1} \right) \\&= \left( - 1000 \right) \cdot 0.04 \cdot \left( \left( - 7 \right) \cdot \cos \left( \operatorname{radians} 60 \right) - 7 \right) - 1000 \cdot 0.08 \cdot \left( 7 \cdot \cos \left( \operatorname{radians} 60 \right) - 7 \right) \\&= 721.58 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R_{y} &= \rho_{water} \cdot Q_{A} \cdot \left( \left( - v_{1} \right) \cdot \sin \left( \operatorname{radians} \theta \right) - 0 \right) + \rho_{water} \cdot Q_{B} \cdot \left( v_{1} \cdot \sin \left( \operatorname{radians} \theta \right) - 0 \right) \\&= 1000 \cdot 0.04 \cdot \left( \left( - 7 \right) \cdot \sin \left( \operatorname{radians} 60 \right) - 0 \right) + 1000 \cdot 0.08 \cdot \left( 7 \cdot \sin \left( \operatorname{radians} 60 \right) - 0 \right) \\&= 249.96 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] R &= \sqrt { \left( R_{x} \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( R_{y} \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= \sqrt { \left( 721.58 \right) ^{ 2 } + \left( 249.96 \right) ^{ 2 } } \\&= 763.65 \; \;\textrm{(N)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \theta_{F} &= \operatorname{degrees} \left( \arctan \left( \frac{ R_{y} }{ R_{x} } \right) \right) \\&= \operatorname{degrees} \left( \arctan \left( \frac{ 249.96 }{ 721.58 } \right) \right) \\&= 19.11 \; \;\textrm{(°)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Question 1 (**)[R]#

A 2 km long, 500 mm diameter pipeline carries water at a velocity of 2 m/s. Calculate the pressure rise in the system if water assuming a rigid pipe and a water temperature of 15 °Celsius. {298 m}

Given:

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} D &= 500 \; \;\textrm{(mm)} &L &= 2 \; \;\textrm{(km)} &V &= 2 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)} \\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Hint

To calculate the pressure rise in the system the water hammer equation can be used.

\[\begin{split} c= \sqrt {E_v}{\rho_{water}}\\ \Delta p= \rho c V\\ H= \frac {\Delta p}{\rho_{water} g} \end{split}\]

The bulk modulus of elasticity can be found in Table A.1 for 15°C \(E_v= 2.14 x10^6 kN/m^2\)

Solution

Show code cell outputs Hide code cell outputs
\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E_{v} &= 2140000000.00 \; \;\textrm{(N/m2)} \\[10pt] c &= \sqrt { \frac{ E_{v} }{ \rho_{water} } } \\&= \sqrt { \frac{ 2140000000.00 }{ 1000 } } \\&= 1462.87 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \Delta_{P} &= \rho_{water} \cdot c \cdot V \\&= 1000 \cdot 1462.87 \cdot 2 \\&= 2925747.77 \; \;\textrm{(Pa)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] H &= \frac{ \Delta_{P} }{ \rho_{water} \cdot g } \\&= \frac{ 2925747.77 }{ 1000 \cdot 9.81 } \\&= 298.24 \; \;\textrm{(m)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Calculate the celerity of a pressure wave in water at 20 °C assuming a water density of 1000 kg/m3 . What difference does it make to your answer if change in water density with temperature is taken into account? {1476.48 m/s; 1477.81 m/s, thus less than 0.1% difference}

\[ \begin{aligned} T &= 20 \; \;\textrm{(°C)} &\rho &= 1000 \; \;\textrm{(kg/m3)} \end{aligned} \]

To calculate the celerity the hammer equation can be used.

\[ c= \sqrt {\frac{E_v}{\rho}} \]

From Table A.1, the density of water at 20 °C is \(998.2 kg/m^3\)
From Table A.4, \(E_v= 1030 x10^6 N/m^2\)

The effect of the density can be calculated with the difference between the celerities:

\[ \text{difference} = \left( \frac{c_2 - c_1}{c_2} \right) 100 \]

Solution

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \rho_{20} &= 998.20 \; \;\textrm{(kg/m3)} \\[10pt] E_{v} &= 2180000000.00 \; \;\textrm{(N/m2)} \\[10pt] c_{1} &= \sqrt { \frac{ E_{v} }{ \rho } } \\&= \sqrt { \frac{ 2180000000.00 }{ 1000 } } \\&= 1476.48 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] c_{2} &= \sqrt { \frac{ E_{v} }{ \rho_{20} } } \\&= \sqrt { \frac{ 2180000000.00 }{ 998.20 } } \\&= 1477.81 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \mathrm{difference} &= \left( \frac{ c_{2} - c_{1} }{ c_{2} } \right) \cdot 100 \\&= \left( \frac{ 1477.81 - 1476.48 }{ 1477.81 } \right) \cdot 100 \\&= 0.09 \; \;\textrm{(%)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Question 3 (**)[R]#

Find the celerity of a pressure wave in benzene if a rigid pipe is assumed. If the initial flow velocity is 2 m/s, what will the increased pressure and pressure head be if a valve in the pipe is suddenly closed? {1081.88 m/s; 1.9 MPa; 220.57 m benzene}

Given:

\[ \begin{aligned} V &= 2 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)} \end{aligned} \]

Hint

To calculate the pressure rise in the system the water hammer equation can be used.

\[\begin{split} c= \sqrt {E_v}{\rho_{water}}\\ \Delta p= \rho c V\\ \end{split}\]

After calculating the pressure increase, the next step is to determine the pressure head

\[ H= \frac {\Delta p}{\rho_{water} g} \]

In Table A.4 for benzene S= 0.88 and \(E_v= 1030 x10^6 N/m^2\)

Solution

Show code cell outputs Hide code cell outputs
\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} S &= 0.88 \; \\[10pt] \rho_{benz} &= S \cdot \rho_{water} \\&= 0.88 \cdot 1000 \\&= 880.00 \; \;\textrm{(kg/m3)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] E_{v} &= 1030000000 \; \;\textrm{(N/m2)} \\[10pt] c &= \sqrt { \frac{ E_{v} }{ S \cdot \rho_{water} } } \\&= \sqrt { \frac{ 1030000000 }{ 0.88 \cdot 1000 } } \\&= 1081.88 \; \;\textrm{(m/s)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \Delta_{P} &= \rho_{benz} \cdot c \cdot V \\&= 880.00 \cdot 1081.88 \cdot 2 \\&= 1904100.84 \; \;\textrm{(Pa)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] \Delta_{P_{Mpa}} &= \frac{ \Delta_{P} }{ 1000000 } \\&= \frac{ 1904100.84 }{ 1000000 } \\&= 1.90 \; \;\textrm{(MPa)}\\[10pt] \\[10pt] H &= \frac{ \Delta_{P} }{ \rho_{benz} \cdot g } \\&= \frac{ 1904100.84 }{ 880.00 \cdot 9.81 } \\&= 220.57 \; \;\textrm{(m)}\\[10pt] \end{aligned} \end{split}\]
